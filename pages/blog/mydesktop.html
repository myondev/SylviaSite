My desktop (and various other devices)

Youmu, looking at you from the side. She's so cool...

I'm going to start talking about my computer here. "WHY?!" you might be asking?
I don't actually know. Other than I'm trying to figure out what this site is about.

As you can see, I'm running Linux. but it's not your run of the mill distro, it's Gentoo. It's the distro where you have to compile all of your apps and build it all from (almost) nothing Why gentoo? Uh...
A lot of cool people I know run it, and it just seems cool to me. As for how it feels to use? Hm. It works as a computer. I can't really say much otherwise.
It feels like exactly how Fedora feels, except I have to wait awhile for software to compile on every update.
Which is fine, I just leave it on while I'm away for school. Maybe I should just use Debian... Nah! Gentoo's cool and it works. The distro shouldn't even be something I'd need to care about anyways. It's not Windows 11, and that's all that matters.
Anyways my desktop. I am using KDE Plasma. We're going with a monochrome theme here. Nothing much, just something I whipped up in like 5 minutes because I wanted something that looks cool but was simple.
There's no icons on my desktop, but that's because I never have my desktop up. It's faster for me to open up the Plasma menu (I use the full screen one) and type in whatever I need to open. Much faster than hiding every window, moving my mouse to whatever icon, and double clicking.

Now then. How'd I make this whole setup?
The panel is Panel Colorizer. All it does is let me change the style of whatever elements are on the panel. Which in this case, is hiding the panel itself (and the spacers), and adding a background to all the little widgets on there.
The icons themselves are from Yet Another Monochrome Icon Set. What kinda name is that? Is there really that many monochrome icon sets that its become a joke? It's pretty cool though.
It has icons for almost every piece of software I use, like Unity or Audacity, hell even LibreWolf! I don't know how they did it. Surely they didn't painstakenly draw it all by hand? Or maybe they did. I wouldn't even know that many apps off the top of my head if i were drawing them.
The Plasma style, Application style, and window decorations are just the basic Breeze Dark ones. Couldn't find anything good, didn't feel like spending that much time, and they already look nice anyways.
The 2 widgets I added have are Kurve, an audio visualizer, and Plasmusic Toolbar, which lets me control my music.
I don't really need them, but they look nice. It also lets me show off whatever songs I'm listening to while recording my game dev logs.
I wish there was something like Rainmeter for Linux.[1] Maybe I'd have my desktop open more if that was the case.

This case... is ancient!!

This is my pc. I named it Alice.
Why Alice? It's a reference to the Touhou Project character ofcourse! Why is it a reference? You'll see why in a bit.
The disc drive is pushed in as you can see... Well while I was cleaning it for the first time in 5 years (yes, this is bad), I had to remove the front panel, which was where the dust covers were.
For some reason, it wouldn't go back on. Because the space between the disc drive and the hole was too tiny. And I was too lazy to fix that, so it's probably gonna stay this way, forever.
This computer also has a ton of history in general. It was previously owned by my sister, then she got a gaming laptop and stopped using it. At the time, I was in desperate need of a computer.
This was coming from a Lenovo C460 All in One which had a Haswell Intel Pentium and integrated graphics, which was AWFUL for me at the time since I was such a huge gamer. So, being me, I just straight up stole it.
It's seen quite a few upgrades since then. Hardly even the same computer at this point.[2] I upgraded the GPU twice, then I upgraded the motherboard and CPU.
